Getting Started

Welcome to Bug Killer, a web game designed to help computer science students learn debugging techniques. This page provides an introduction to the game and some basic useful information.

Introduction

In Bug Killer you play as a computer science student, working as an intern for Denseware, the world's worst software company. Your goal is to survive until the end of summer, when you can return to school.

How to Register

Bug Killer uses Google Accounts to handle registration and logins. If you do not have a Google account, you can create one now. After you have created a Google account and used it to sign in, you must register for Bug Killer separately. This is a quick and simple process. All this does is add you to our database so we can record your progress in the game.

How to Play

The gameplay consists of a series of levels (Accessed throughout the Available Problems page), each of which contains a programming problem. In each problem, you are given a buggy C++ program, and an explanation of what it should do. Your job, as an intern at Denseware, is to fix the buggy program. If you have trouble solving a problem, you can seek help from other players, and each problem also has hints which can help you solve it.

Teams

You are not alone in your quest to fix buggy code, however. Through the Join a Different Team page, you can join a team of other players. On the Team Rankings page, you can see how well your team is performing compared to the other teams, and how well you're performing compared to the rest of your team. Also, because Bug Killer uses email addresses as user names, the team statistics page makes it easy to contact other players on your team when you need help with a problem.
